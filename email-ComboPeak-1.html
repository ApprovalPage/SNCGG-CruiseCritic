One Seattle stop worth building your whole pre-cruise day around

Alaska may be the destination, but Seattle deserves more than a rushed morning.

Before you sail, two iconic experiences sit side by side, ready to turn a few spare hours into the kind of memories that stay with you long after the cruise ends.

At the Space Needle, step onto the world's only revolving glass floor and ride the double-deck glass elevator for a full 360-degree view of the city you're about to leave behind. Then walk next door to Chihuly Garden and Glass, where gallery after gallery bursts with color and light — and every turn feels like something entirely new.

 

Big-deal moments. One easy stop. No complicated logistics.

Getting there is easy. A complimentary shuttle runs from both Pier 91 and Pier 66. Riders take the Traxx shuttle, with direct drop-offs at Seattle Center and Pike Place Market — no transfers, no hassle.

Carve out the time. You'll be glad you did.
